The Great Lakes road trip doesn't have the cultural cachet of Route 66 or the coastal highway loops that dominate travel content. It should. We drove a 12-day loop from Chicago that touched four of the five lakes and came back surprised by how much the Midwest had been underselling itself.

## The Route We Actually Drove

**Chicago → Milwaukee → Door County → Upper Peninsula (Michigan) → Mackinac Island → Traverse City → Detroit → Cleveland → back to Chicago**

Total distance: roughly 2,200 miles. Comfortable for 12 days with a few rest days built in.

## Milwaukee: Don't Underestimate It

Two hours north of Chicago on I-94, Milwaukee is an easy drive-through that deserves a full overnight. The city's brewing heritage is real — Lakefront Brewery does tours ($12, worth it) and the beer culture here is unpretentious in the best way.

The Milwaukee Art Museum is the visual anchor of the lakefront, housed in a Santiago Calatrava building with a brise soleil (movable wing structure) that opens and closes daily. The building is the attraction, but the collection — particularly the Wisconsin Wisconsin collection — is better than you'd expect.

Budget lodging options in Milwaukee are genuinely good value: solid hotels at $90-130/night that would be $200+ in Chicago.

## Door County: Exactly What the Photos Suggest

Door County, Wisconsin — the peninsula jutting into Lake Michigan — delivers on its reputation for cherry orchards, small harbor towns, and slower-paced lake life. We spent two nights in Fish Creek and it was the most relaxed stretch of the whole trip.

The water is clear enough to see the bottom in the shallower bays. Kayak rental runs around $35/half-day. Al Johnson's Swedish Restaurant in Sister Bay (goats on the roof, as advertised) had a 45-minute wait but the Swedish pancakes were worth it.

## The Upper Peninsula: The Trip's Best Surprise

Nobody had adequately prepared me for Michigan's Upper Peninsula. We came in via the Mackinac Bridge and spent three nights, which wasn't enough.

**Pictured Rocks National Lakeshore** — multicolored sandstone cliffs above Lake Superior, accessible by kayak or boat tour. Boat tours from Munising run about $40-50 and cover 15 miles of cliffs. The colors — rust, green, white, ochre — look digitally enhanced in photos but they're real. Lake Superior is cold enough in August to make swimming interesting.

**Tahquamenon Falls** — called the "root beer falls" for the tannin-colored water. Not Niagara, but genuinely beautiful, and you can rent rowboats to reach the upper falls from below.

The UP has a character distinct from lower Michigan — more isolated, more logging-and-mining heritage, more Finnish influence (seriously, pasties are everywhere and they're excellent). Towns are small and services are sparse, which means gas up when you can.

## Traverse City: Michigan's Wine Country Surprise

Traverse City sits on Grand Traverse Bay on Lake Michigan's eastern shore and is the center of Michigan's wine country, which makes more sense than it sounds — the lakes moderate temperatures, producing conditions that work well for cold-climate varietals like Riesling and Pinot Noir.

We did a half-day on the Old Mission Peninsula, stopping at three wineries. The wines range from good to excellent. Old Mission Peninsula Winery, Bowers Harbor Vineyards, and Chateau Chantal were all solid. The landscape — water on both sides of the narrow peninsula — is scenic in a way that doesn't require the wine to justify the drive.

## Detroit: More Interesting Than You've Been Told

Detroit's reputation arrives before the city does. The industrial decay narrative is real but increasingly incomplete. The city's recovery has been genuinely creative — the Midtown and Corktown neighborhoods have real energy, and the Detroit Institute of Arts is among the best art museums in the United States.

The Diego Rivera murals in the DIA's Rivera Court — depicting Ford's River Rouge complex workers — are one of the great American artworks. They're also free to see with museum admission (~$14).

For food: Corktown's Slows Bar BQ, Lafayette Coney Island for the obligatory coney dog experience, Supino Pizzeria in Eastern Market for what might be the best pizza in the Midwest.

## What We'd Change

We moved too fast between Milwaukee and Door County and too slow getting through Ohio. Reverse that ratio next time. Also: the UP deserves five nights minimum if you're serious about Pictured Rocks.

Ohio surprised us repeatedly — the Lake Erie islands (particularly South Bass Island and Put-in-Bay) are a genuinely fun lake destination that we barely had time for. That's a whole separate trip.

---

*Best time: late June through early September. The UP can be cold even in July — pack accordingly. Book Door County accommodations months in advance for summer weekends.*
