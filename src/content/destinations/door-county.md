## Discovering Door County

Door County is the thumb of Wisconsin's mitten hand, a 70-mile peninsula between Green Bay and Lake Michigan with 11 lighthouses, 19 state parks and natural areas, hundreds of miles of shoreline, and a summer season that draws nearly 3 million visitors to a county of 30,000 year-round residents. The villages — Ephraim, Fish Creek, Sister Bay, Baileys Harbor — have kept their 19th-century fishing village character while developing genuinely excellent restaurants and galleries.

The fish boil is non-negotiable. Swedish settlers developed the tradition and it involves boiling lake whitefish with potatoes over a wood fire, finishing with a dramatic kerosene flare-up that creates the boilover — a literal boiling-over moment that the crowd watches from a safe distance. The White Gull Inn's Thursday night fish boil, served with Door County cherry pie, is the canonical version.

October brings fall color that rivals Vermont without Vermont's crowds. July brings cherry harvest and pick-your-own orchards throughout the peninsula. June is the locals' secret — everything is open and the summer tourists haven't quite arrived.
